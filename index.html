How deadlines are calculated (O 2, ROC 2021 + Interpretation Act)

  • Trigger day excluded: The period begins on the day after the triggering event (Interpretation Act s 50(a)).
  • Periods of 5 days or fewer (O 2 r 3): Saturdays, Sundays, and public holidays are excluded from the count (effectively working days).
  • Periods over 5 days: All calendar days count; months and weeks are calendar months/weeks.
  • Last-day rule: If the final day falls on a weekend or public holiday, it extends to the next working day. For Supreme Court proceedings, if the final day falls on a court vacation day, it likewise extends to the next working day (non-weekend/non-public holiday) — which may still fall within a vacation period. Adjusted deadlines are marked ⚠.
  • State Courts: Court vacation days have no effect. Only the ≤5 day working-day rule and the last-day rule (weekends/public holidays only) apply.
  • Cutoff deadlines (shown in brown): Some deadlines run backwards from a future date (e.g. last day to amend before trial). If the cutoff falls on a non-filing day, the preceding filing day is shown.
